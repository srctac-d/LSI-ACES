Modular Self-Tightening Isotropic Structural Truss Matrix with Integrated Vanadium Energy Management System
SECTION I: STRUCTURAL INNOVATION DISCLOSURE
Isotropic Self-Tightening Woven Truss (IST-Truss)
1. Fundamental Mechanism

A planar-woven, modified arch framework adapted from structural Hongqiao dynamics. The system utilizes 100% identical, non-handed (isotropic) structural ribs interlocked alongside continuous, straight longitudinal runners. Downward floor/deck mass and atmospheric/hydrostatic pressures force the crossing ribs into a scissor-action pivot, converting live loads directly into radial clamping forces across the runners and joints.

Key Geometric & Node SpecificationsCenter Pivot Node: A primary alignment/shear pin situated at the exact geometric center where crossing ribs intersect.Secondary Torque-Lock Pins: Two secondary pins situated at $0.25 \times \text{rib width}$ along the rotational radius from the center pin. Fluid Containment & Structural Ballast: The liquid Vanadium electrolyte tanks act as low-center-of-gravity fluid ballast within the structural base of the truss frame (e.g., in houseboat hulls or off-grid trailers), improving roll stability while simultaneously delivering primary grid power.

    Load-Driven Structural Tightening: Downward mass from battery tanks, floor decks, and internal utility modules actively increases friction at the pinned truss nodes, turning stored power mass into structural clamping force.

    Protected Conduit Channels: The natural voids generated by the crossing ribs create an armor-shielded internal raceway for Vanadium fluid plumbing, optical fiber telemetry, and high-voltage power lines.
